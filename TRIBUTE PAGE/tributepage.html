Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India & People's President

Why I Admire Him

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was not only a distinguished scientist but also a great visionary and a humble human being. His life is an inspiration for millions of young people. Coming from a modest background, he rose to become the 11th President of India and earned the title of the People’s President.

I deeply admire Dr. Kalam for his simplicity, dedication to education, and his ability to dream big and inspire others to do the same. His words, "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action." continue to motivate me every day.

Early Life & Education

Dr. Kalam was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Coming from a humble background, he sold newspapers as a boy to support his family. His love for learning led him to study physics at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, and later aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.

Scientific Achievements

  • Developed India’s first satellite launch vehicle SLV-III.
  • Key contributor to Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Known as the Missile Man of India for missile technology contributions.
  • Served as Chief Scientific Adviser & Principal Scientific Adviser to Govt. of India.

As the President of India

In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the 11th President of India. His tenure was marked by humility, accessibility, and a deep connection with the youth. He was called the People’s President.
